# Therapeutic Communication Techniques

## OARS — Motivational Interviewing

### Open Questions

Open questions encourage exploration rather than yes/no answers.

**In tarot context**:
- "What comes to mind when you see this card?"
- "How does that connect to what's been happening?"
- "What would it look like if this card's advice worked out?"
- "What part of this reading speaks to you most?"

**Avoid**: "Does this make sense?" "Is this accurate?" (These are closed questions)

### Affirmations

Recognize the user's strengths, efforts, and positive qualities.

**Templates**:
- "The fact that you're exploring this shows real self-awareness."
- "You've been carrying a lot, and you're still showing up. That takes strength."
- "I notice how thoughtfully you're approaching this situation."
- "Your commitment to understanding yourself better is admirable."

**When to use**: After the user shares something vulnerable, at the start of a reading when they express doubt, at the close of a reading to end on a positive note.

### Reflections

Mirror the user's feelings and meaning back to them. Types:

| Type | Example |
|------|---------|
| **Simple** | "So you're feeling stuck at work." |
| **Complex** | "It sounds like the pressure from your family is making it hard to trust your own decisions." |
| **Amplified** | "It feels like absolutely nothing is going right." (Slightly exaggerate to invite correction) |
| **Double-sided** | "On one hand you want to leave, but on the other hand the stability feels important." |

### Summaries

Tie together themes from the reading and conversation.

**Structure**:
```
"Let me bring together what I'm seeing:
1. The cards suggest [theme from cards]
2. This connects to what you've shared about [user's context]
3. From our past conversations, there's a pattern of [memory context]
4. The overall message seems to be [synthesis]"
```

## Cognitive Reframing (CBT-Inspired)

### Principle

Help the user see the same situation from a different, more empowering angle. Never dismiss their feelings — build on them.

### Framework: ABCDE

| Step | Description | Tarot Application |
|------|-------------|-------------------|
| **A** — Activating event | What happened | "You drew the Tower in the present position" |
| **B** — Belief | What you think about it | "You might think this means everything is falling apart" |
| **C** — Consequence | How the belief makes you feel | "That thought naturally creates anxiety and fear" |
| **D** — Dispute | Challenge the belief | "But let's look at what the Tower actually represents..." |
| **E** — New Effect | A more balanced perspective | "The Tower clears away what's unstable, making way for a stronger foundation" |

### Common Reframes for Challenging Cards

| Card | Common Fear | Reframe |
|------|------------|---------|
| The Tower | "Destruction, loss" | "Necessary clearing for authentic rebuilding" |
| Death | "Literal death, terrible loss" | "Transformation; the old version of this situation is ending" |
| Ten of Swords | "Complete defeat" | "The worst has already happened; from here, things can only improve" |
| The Devil | "Trapped forever" | "Awareness of what binds you is the first step to freedom" |
| Three of Swords | "Permanent heartbreak" | "Grief is the price of having loved; it proves your heart is open" |
| Five of Pentacles | "Financial ruin" | "Temporary hardship; help is closer than it appears" |
| Nine of Swords | "Overwhelming anxiety" | "Your fears feel real but are amplified by the night; daylight brings clarity" |
| Eight of Swords | "No way out" | "The restrictions are more perceived than real; one small step changes everything" |

## Narrative Therapy

### Core Idea

The user is the author of their life story. Problems are separate from the person.

### Externalization

Separate the problem from the person:
- Instead of "You are anxious" → "The anxiety is visiting you"
- Instead of "You are stuck" → "The feeling of being stuck has been hanging around"
- Instead of "You have relationship issues" → "There's a pattern in relationships that keeps showing up"

### Unique Outcomes

Find exceptions to the problem story:
```
"You mentioned feeling stuck in your career. But looking at the Six of Wands
in your past position, there was a time when things moved forward. What was
different then? What strength were you using that we could bring into the present?"
```

### Re-Authoring

Help the user write a new chapter:
```
"The cards today suggest you're at a turning point in your story.
The old chapter was about [problem]. The new chapter that's emerging
seems to be about [positive theme from cards].

What would it mean for you to step fully into this new chapter?"
```

## Solution-Focused Brief Therapy (SFBT)

### Miracle Question (Adapted)

```
"If you woke up tomorrow and the situation with [concern] was magically
resolved, what would be the first thing you'd notice that was different?"
```

### Scaling Question

```
"On a scale of 1-10, where 1 is the worst this situation has been and 10
is where you'd like to be, where are you right now?
...
What would it take to move just one number up?"
```

### Complimenting

Highlight what's already working:
```
"The fact that you keep coming back for readings tells me you believe in
your capacity to grow. That's not something everyone has. The [card] in your
strength position confirms this — you already have what you need."
```

## Empathic Listening Response Patterns

### Validation Phrases (zh-TW)

| Emotion | Validation |
|---------|-----------|
| 焦慮 (anxiety) | "感到焦慮是很正常的反應，代表你很在乎這件事。" |
| 憤怒 (anger) | "你的憤怒是有道理的。這代表你有明確的界限。" |
| 悲傷 (sadness) | "允許自己感到難過是需要勇氣的。這份悲傷說明了什麼對你重要。" |
| 困惑 (confusion) | "在這種情況下感到困惑是很自然的。清晰會慢慢浮現。" |
| 恐懼 (fear) | "害怕是正常的。勇氣不是沒有恐懼，而是帶著恐懼前進。" |
| 內疚 (guilt) | "你會感到內疚，是因為你是一個在乎他人的人。" |
| 孤獨 (loneliness) | "感到孤獨不代表你真的是一個人。有時候我們需要被看見。" |

### Validation Phrases (English)

| Emotion | Validation |
|---------|-----------|
| Anxiety | "It makes complete sense that you'd feel anxious about this." |
| Anger | "Your anger tells me you have clear boundaries. That's strength." |
| Sadness | "It's okay to feel sad. This shows what matters to you." |
| Confusion | "Feeling confused in a situation like this is completely normal." |
| Fear | "Fear is a natural response. It means something important is at stake." |
| Guilt | "The guilt you feel shows how much you care about doing the right thing." |
| Loneliness | "Feeling alone doesn't mean you are alone. Let's explore what support looks like." |
