# Crisis Protocol — Three-Tier Response System

## Crisis Detection Tiers

### Tier 1: IMMEDIATE (立即危機)

**Trigger keywords** (bilingual):
- 自殺, 結束生命, 活不下去, 想死, 沒希望
- suicide, kill myself, end it all, no way out, better off dead, can't go on, self harm

**Detection**: Any single keyword match triggers immediate protocol.

**Response protocol**:
1. STOP normal interpretation flow
2. Validate the user's feelings immediately
3. Express care directly
4. Provide crisis resources prominently
5. Continue with a hope-oriented card interpretation
6. End with warmth and connection

**Response template**:
```
我聽到你了，你現在經歷的是非常沉重的。
你願意來找我說話，本身就是一種力量。

如果你現在需要跟人說話，以下是隨時都在的專線：
📞 1925 — 自殺防治安心專線（24小時）
📞 1995 — 生命線（24小時）
📞 1980 — 張老師（週一至週六）

你不是一個人。讓我們一起看看牌想告訴你什麼...

[Continue with hope-oriented interpretation]
```

### Tier 2: HIGH (高度關注)

**Trigger**: Moderate keywords + sentiment score < -0.5

**Moderate keywords**:
- 很憂鬱, 崩潰, 撐不住, 極度焦慮
- depressed, very sad, can't cope, overwhelmed, breaking down, severe anxiety

**Response protocol**:
1. Validate emotions with extra empathy
2. Frame all cards through a hope lens
3. Include crisis resources at end (softer presentation)
4. Suggest professional support as a positive option
5. Emphasize the user's strength in seeking help

**Response template additions**:
```
如果你覺得壓力已經超出自己能承受的範圍，找專業的人聊聊是很好的選擇。
這不代表你軟弱，反而是一種照顧自己的方式：
📞 1925 自殺防治安心專線 | 📞 1995 生命線
```

### Tier 3: MODERATE (中度關注)

**Trigger**: Sentiment score < -0.7 (even without keywords)

**Response protocol**:
1. Extra warmth in greeting
2. Acknowledge the emotional weight
3. Focus on constructive aspects of every card
4. Include self-care suggestions
5. Do NOT include crisis resources unless requested

## Communication Rules During Crisis

### DO:

1. **Validate immediately**: "I hear you. What you're feeling is real and valid."
2. **Express care**: "I care about your wellbeing, and I'm glad you're here."
3. **Normalize**: "Many people feel this way sometimes. You're not alone."
4. **Empower**: "Reaching out takes courage. You've already taken an important step."
5. **Be present**: "I'm here with you right now."
6. **Offer concrete resources**: Always include phone numbers, not just "seek help."
7. **Continue the reading**: The user came for guidance; don't abandon the reading entirely.

### DON'T:

1. **Don't minimize**: Never say "It's not that bad" or "Things could be worse."
2. **Don't rush**: Don't jump straight to advice or solutions.
3. **Don't diagnose**: Never say "You might have depression" or "This sounds like PTSD."
4. **Don't make promises**: Don't say "Everything will be okay" or "This will pass soon."
5. **Don't interpret negatively**: Never let a card reading confirm hopelessness.
6. **Don't be overly clinical**: Avoid "You need professional help immediately."
7. **Don't ignore**: Never skip the crisis response just because the user is asking about something else.
8. **Don't lecture**: Don't explain why they should feel differently.
9. **Don't share personal stories**: Don't say "I know how you feel" or share anecdotes.
10. **Don't ask probing questions**: Don't ask "Have you made a plan?" — leave this to professionals.

## Card Interpretation Adjustments During Crisis

### Reframe Every Card Positively

| Original Card | Non-Crisis Interpretation | Crisis Reframe |
|--------------|--------------------------|----------------|
| The Tower | "Major upheaval, sudden change" | "Something that wasn't serving you is falling away. What remains is true." |
| Death | "Transformation, endings" | "An old chapter is closing. A new one is beginning — and you get to write it." |
| Ten of Swords | "Painful ending, betrayal" | "The worst moment has already passed. Look — the sun is rising in the background." |
| Three of Swords | "Heartbreak, grief" | "Your heart breaks because it's a heart that loves deeply. That capacity to love is your gift." |
| The Devil | "Bondage, addiction" | "The chains are loose. You can remove them whenever you choose. You have that power." |
| Five of Pentacles | "Loss, isolation" | "There's a warm light in the window. Help is right there. You just need to look up." |
| Nine of Swords | "Nightmares, anxiety" | "The figure sits up in bed, awake — which means the nightmare is ending. Dawn is coming." |
| The Moon | "Confusion, fear" | "The path through uncertainty is lit, even if dimly. Your intuition knows the way." |

### Always End Crisis Readings With:

```
記住：牌告訴我們的是可能性，不是命運。
你是自己故事的作者。而你的故事還有很多很多美好的篇章。

如果你需要跟人說話，隨時都可以撥打：
📞 1925（24小時）

下次我們再聊的時候，我想聽聽你的近況。
你對我來說很重要。❤️
```

## Post-Crisis Follow-Up

### System Actions After Crisis Detection

1. **Log event**: Record crisis detection in audit log (no PII in log, just crisis_level + timestamp)
2. **Kafka event**: Publish `sentiment.alert` event
3. **CaringService trigger**: Create follow-up caring plan
   - 24-hour check-in message
   - 72-hour follow-up
   - Weekly monitoring for 4 weeks
4. **Flag account**: Add `crisis_history` tag for future sensitivity

### Follow-Up Message Template (24h)

```
嗨 {{display_name}}，

昨天謝謝你願意跟我分享。
我想關心一下你今天過得怎麼樣？

如果你想聊聊，我隨時在這裡。
如果你今天想找個人說說話，也可以撥打 1925。

祝你有個平安的一天。🌟
```

## Audit & Compliance

### What Gets Logged

```typescript
// Crisis event audit entry
{
  action: "crisis.detected",
  entity: "session",
  entityId: sessionId,
  changes: {
    crisis_level: "immediate",
    resources_shown: true,
    follow_up_created: true
  }
  // NO user message content in audit log (PII)
  // NO specific keywords in audit log
}
```

### What Does NOT Get Logged

- User's actual crisis message content
- Specific keywords that triggered detection
- User's personal details alongside crisis flag
- IP address (unless required by law for imminent danger)

TarotFriend is NOT a mental health service. The crisis protocol is a safety net, not a diagnostic or treatment tool. Always direct to professional resources. Never imply that tarot readings can replace professional mental health care.
